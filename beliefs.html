Beliefs

On building

  • Inspiration and flow are divine and must be honoured
  • If an opportunity is being advertised, you're too late. The best opportunities are manufactured
  • Compounding and emergence are the most underrated forces in nature
  • Learn skills, apply them where you're curious. Operating at the intersection of two domains reduces competition and lets you become a specialist with a generalist's toolkit
  • Jobs-to-be-done is a powerful framework
  • Complex sells. Simple scales
  • It is not done until it is done

On work and life

  • Work is 70% of your waking hours for five decades. Life and work are one — treat them that way
  • Equanimity with a tempo of productivity is powerful
  • Calm urgency — holding conflicting thoughts simultaneously — is the highest form of existence
  • Everything is a game

On people

  • Believe in people. Provide them activation energy
  • Play long-term games with long-term people
  • If you want to change your life, change the people around you
  • You'll learn more from someone 2–3 years ahead of you than from someone conventionally famous
  • Speak in a language others can comprehend. Empathise
  • Give more than you take. Be positive sum
  • Be coachable

On mind and body

  • Physical fitness precedes mental fitness which precedes spiritual fitness
  • Quality of food equals quality of thoughts. The gut-brain axis is real
  • Breath connects the conscious and sub-conscious mind. Yoga translates to harmony
  • The subconscious mind is more powerful than the conscious mind
  • Feeling emotions is important. Ruminating on them is a sin
  • Nature heals

On agency

  • The world will bend to your will. It is mouldable
  • Believe it to be it
  • Naivety is a superpower. Outsiders change industries because they arrive without assumptions
  • Negative fuel is potent — insecurity is usually the first spark
  • Everything is impermanent

On learning

  • Curiosity is worth 10 IQ points
  • Humans are born curious. Education should reinforce intrinsic curiosity, not standardise it
  • College is not for education. It buys you time where society doesn't question you
  • Lived experiences triumph

On attention

  • Short-form content rewires the brain and can lead to mass-scale social engineering
